About EcoConnect

A community-led response to waste pollution

EcoConnect Initiative is a youth-led, community-based project focused on waste management, waterway protection, and restoring polluted spaces through practical local action.

Our work begins with the realities communities already face: open dumping, blocked drainage, polluted waterways, unsafe burning, and limited disposal options. We respond with a system that is simple, participatory, and designed to grow through community trust.

What the initiative combines
Community cleanups Waste collection points Environmental education Hotspot reporting Youth mobilisation Tree planting Waterway protection
Why it matters

The problem we are working to change

Poor waste management affects health, livelihoods, and the condition of local ecosystems.

Waste

Open dumping and poor disposal

Waste is often discarded in open spaces, drainage channels, and near waterways where formal systems are weak or absent.

Waterways

Polluted water and blocked flow

Accumulated waste can pollute water sources, disrupt drainage, and threaten fish, riparian habitats, and the people who depend on them.

Health

Unsafe coping practices

Where disposal options are limited, open burning and unsafe waste handling become common, increasing environmental and health risks.

Our approach

How EcoConnect works

  • 1

    Community-led cleanups

    Regular cleanups help remove waste before it reaches waterways while creating visible entry points for trust-building and action.

  • 2

    Designated collection points

    Collection points make safer disposal more practical and reduce repeated dumping in high-risk areas.

  • 3

    Simple reporting and response

    Residents help identify recurring pollution hotspots so action can be focused where it matters most.

  • 4

    Education and restoration

    Environmental education, youth mobilisation, and tree planting support behaviour change and recovery of degraded spaces over time.

Seed-stage implementation focus

EcoConnect is currently in its early pilot phase. The immediate focus is to establish waste hotspots, introduce simple reporting, work with local waste collectors, and build a practical foundation for safer disposal and waterway protection.

App & data flow

A simple reporting layer that supports community action

EcoConnect’s digital concept is designed to support, not replace, local action. Residents can report recurring waste hotspots, while a simple dashboard helps visualise patterns and guide cleanup response, collection point placement, and future interventions.

At this stage, the focus is on practical early use: community reporting, visible hotspot tracking, and making local environmental information easier to act on.

  • Community-submitted reports of waste accumulation and pollution hotspots
  • Simple dashboard views to support planning and prioritisation
  • Local education content to reinforce safer disposal and environmental awareness

Focus areas

What our work is designed to improve

EcoConnect connects waste action, environmental education, and restoration into one practical system.

Waste management

Reduce dumping at source

By combining collection points, cleanups, and local coordination, EcoConnect aims to make proper disposal easier and more visible.

Waterway protection

Local knowledge • healthier habits
Restoration
